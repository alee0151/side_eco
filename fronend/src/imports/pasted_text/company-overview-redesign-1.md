Redesign only “Page 02 — Company Overview” for EcoTrace to make it significantly more investor-friendly, more information-dense, and visually more creative, while still preserving the brand’s trustworthy, eco-friendly, premium SaaS aesthetic.

Do NOT redesign the whole product.
Do NOT change the left sidebar, top search bar, or overall design language.
Only upgrade the Company Overview page so it feels suitable for investors, ESG analysts, and sustainability teams, while still remaining understandable to non-experts.

Overall visual direction:
- Keep the soft cream or white background.
- Use the existing green + blue palette with yellow/orange accents for warnings.
- Maintain rounded cards, subtle shadows, clear spacing, flat vector icons, and high readability.
- Make the page feel more premium, analytical, and “investment terminal meets modern ESG SaaS”.
- Increase visual sophistication through layered data cards, modular panels, mini visualizations, and richer hierarchy.

Page goal:
Turn the Company Overview page into a high-confidence investor snapshot that explains biodiversity exposure, score drivers, evidence quality, trend movement, and reporting relevance in one screen.

Main layout:
Use a 3-column desktop layout:
- Left: company profile, filters, exposure tags, and quick navigation anchors.
- Center: primary score, trend visuals, key findings, LEAP summary, and material risk drivers.
- Right: evidence summary, confidence breakdown, provenance summary, and “what changed” timeline.

- Coverage status badge (High / Medium / Limited coverage)

- Main biodiversity risk score from 0–100

- 30-day score change

- Peer percentile
- Confidence score

Make this hero more creative:
- Use a large radial score visualization or layered circular gauge.
- Surround it with small orbiting metadata chips like “Protected area overlap”, “Supplier exposure”, “Regulatory signal”, “Species sensitivity”.
- Add a subtle segmented ring to show how the score is composed.

Explain every number shown directly in the UI using short helper text or tooltip-ready microcopy.

Required data explanations:
- Biodiversity Risk Score (0–100): Explain that 0 means very low observed biodiversity risk and 100 means severe or concentrated biodiversity risk signals across operations, locations, or supply chain.
- 30-day change: Explain that this measures whether risk signals increased or decreased compared with the prior 30 days.
- Peer percentile: Explain where the company sits relative to others in the same sector.
- Confidence score: Explain that this reflects strength, consistency, recency, and traceability of the evidence.
- Data coverage: Explain the percentage of expected data sources successfully resolved and analysed.
- Evidence sources: Explain how many documents, records, and structured inputs contributed to the assessment.
- Last updated: Explain the freshness of the intelligence.
- Trend chart: Explain whether the company’s biodiversity profile is improving, stable, or deteriorating.
- Exposure metrics: Explain what part of the company’s footprint intersects with sensitive ecosystems, species areas, or supplier-linked hotspots.
